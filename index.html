Croissant

The croissant began as the Austrian kipfel but became French the moment people began to make it with puffed pastry, which is a French innovation. The secret of an excellent croissant is the quality of the ingredients: sugar, salt, flour, milk, eggs, and of course, butter. The curved croissant (made with margarine) is called a croissant ordinaire and is now less common than the straight one (made with butter). We serve both shapes with different tastes.
